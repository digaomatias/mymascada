# Smart Debt Management Strategies

## Know Your Debt Types
**Good Debt** (typically tax-deductible, helps build wealth):
- Mortgages
- Student loans
- Business loans

**Bad Debt** (high interest, no tax benefits):
- Credit card debt
- Personal loans
- Auto loans (usually)

## Two Popular Payoff Strategies

### The Debt Avalanche (Mathematically Optimal)
1. Pay minimums on all debts
2. Put extra money toward highest interest rate debt
3. When that's paid off, move to next highest rate
4. **Best for**: Saving the most money in interest

### The Debt Snowball (Psychologically Motivating)
1. Pay minimums on all debts
2. Put extra money toward smallest balance
3. When that's paid off, move to next smallest balance
4. **Best for**: Building momentum and staying motivated

## Smart Debt Management Tips

### Credit Cards
- Pay the full balance every month if possible
- If you can't, pay more than the minimum
- Use autopay to avoid late fees
- Consider balance transfers for lower rates (watch transfer fees)

### Student Loans
- Know if they're federal or private
- Explore income-driven repayment plans
- Consider refinancing private loans for better rates
- Don't ignore them - they rarely go away

### Mortgages
- Extra principal payments can save thousands in interest
- Consider refinancing when rates drop significantly
- Avoid borrowing against home equity for consumer purchases

## Red Flags
- Only making minimum payments on everything
- Using credit cards for basic necessities
- Taking cash advances
- Borrowing from retirement accounts
- Having no emergency fund while carrying debt

## Emergency Fund vs. Debt Payoff
**Build a small emergency fund first** ($500-$1000), then focus on debt payoff. This prevents new debt when unexpected expenses arise.
